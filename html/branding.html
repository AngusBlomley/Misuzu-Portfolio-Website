To promote the sixth-sector industrialization (The sixth-sector industrialization is an initiative that integrates agriculture, forestry, and fisheries (primary industry), manufacturing (secondary industry), and sales and services (tertiary industry) to create higher added value and revitalize local economies) in Japan's Tohoku region, specifically in Iwate Prefecture, a branding project was carried out aimed at revitalizing the area using "Kuromame," a type of black bean cultivated by local farmers. Based on this produce, product development was conducted, and packaging designs and a website were created.

"Kuromame OMAME no CAKE" is a unique DIY pound cake kit featuring Kuromame (black beans). This kit includes a special mix of blended flour and Kuromame, along with sweetened Kuromame. Everything needed for cake-making is neatly packed in a user-friendly box, including a spoon for mixing, as well as a knife, fork, and placemat for enjoying the freshly baked cake. With this kit, you can easily create a delicious Kuromame pound cake at home without the need to gather additional ingredients. "Kuromame OMAME no CAKE" offers a simple and tasty homemade baking experience, highlighting the quality of its ingredients.

Design

The design of this magazine emphasizes showcasing the Majolica tiles originating from Mallorca Island. It features soft background colors to highlight the beauty of the tiles. The visuals are boldly scattered across the pages, adding dynamism to the layout. To aid comprehension, lengthy texts have been distributed across different topics, making the content more reader-friendly and accessible.
